How we met

Lindsay and Bailey met at a small sandwich shop in Northfield, Minnesota during the 2012 Carleton women's soccer preseason. It has recently been declassified that at first Lindsay confused Bailey for another player on the team (a pet peeve of Bailey's). During their three overlapping years at Carleton College, they crossed paths frequently in places 'to see and be seen' such as: Bell Field (where Lindsay often antagonized the coach), in the Classics lounge (where Bailey hogged the only table despite not being a Classics student), and other local haunts like the library, the basement of Hill House, the Arb, and Froggy Bottoms Pub ("Froggy's" for short).

Their relationship blossomed furtively spring term of Bailey's senior year (did you know they learned morse code together?) and continued despite the five years of long-distance they navigated afterwards. Throughout the eight years they've been together, they've traveled across Europe and Turkey, they've taken way too many red-eye flights to Iceland to bridge the long-distance gap (anyone remember WOW! Airlines?), they've learned how to surf and boulder, they've trained for ultra marathons, and they've collected a library's worth of books. Although none of this would have happened without their deep bond (quantum entanglement?), it would also not have been nearly as much fun without their large network of friends and family, as well as their beloved canine.

Bailey proposed to Lindsay surrounded by some of their closest friends in California. Lindsay counter-proposed 6 months later while they were surfing, just before a wave crashed on them. They can't wait to celebrate their love with you.

About the Venue

Josephine Butler Parks Center is a three-story historic embassy building turned non-profit parks center with wood fireplaces and a beautiful balcony overlooking Malcolm X Park. The Parks Center's work is focused on restoring D.C. parks and green spaces, sponsoring new environmental projects like urban farms, and leading a volunteer environmental clean-up program called Green Corps. Its mission is inspired by its namesake, a racial justice leader who championed access to clean air, water, and nature for all communities in D.C. We picked this venue in part because our money will go right back to parks programming for communities in the area.
Because the venue is a historic site, there are no elevators. However, both the ceremony and reception will be held on the same floor and we will have people ready to assist.
